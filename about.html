About

Nikolympia Games — who, what, and why.

Who runs this

I'm Nikos. Nikolympia is an independent project: I design and build browser games I actually want to play — quick to start, no 2GB download, no forced account. You open a tab, tap play, and you're in.

Everything here runs with HTML, JavaScript, and Canvas (and a bit of CSS love). The goal is lightweight pages that still feel like real games on desktop or phone.

What's on the site

The home hub lists every live title with links, short descriptions, and live leaderboards so you can see real top scores before you play.

Void Survivors

Arcade survival roguelike: you auto-attack through waves of enemies, pick up weapons, evolve them, and face a boss every 10 levels. Runs can be a few minutes or much longer. Your score can post to a global leaderboard under the name you choose.

Up or Lose

Endless vertical climber with auto-jump: moving platforms, boosts, teleports, and ways to fail fast. Sessions are usually short — perfect for “one more run.” Leaderboard tracks height (and bragging rights).

Ascension Protocol

A longer-form idle / incremental game: phases, multiple currencies, upgrades, and a sci-fi AI theme. Built for people who like optimizing production and watching numbers go up over days, not just single sprints.

Fair play & usernames

Leaderboards are for real players. Offensive usernames, obvious cheats, or abuse can get entries removed. There’s no pay-to-win on the hub — progression is from playing, not from paying us.

How the site is funded

The site is intended to stay free to play. Hosting and time cost money, so we use advertising (Google AdSense) where appropriate. Details on cookies, ads, and data are in the Privacy Policy.

Why browser games?

No install means anyone with a modern browser can play — school laptop, phone on the bus, or a proper gaming PC. Canvas and today’s browsers can do a lot more than old stereotypes suggest.

Contact

Bugs, ideas, or “this broke on my phone” — use the contact page. I read what people send.
